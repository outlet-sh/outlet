# Financial Services Firm Implements AI Governance in 6 Weeks

## The Challenge

A wealth management firm with $2.1B AUM and 180 employees faced a dilemma:

**The pressure to adopt AI was real:**

- Competitors were deploying AI for client research and portfolio analysis
- Advisors were already using ChatGPT on personal devices
- Leadership wanted to pilot AI tools but was afraid of compliance risks

**The fear was equally real:**

- SEC and FINRA scrutiny of AI usage in financial services
- Client data privacy concerns (PII, financial information)
- Risk of AI "hallucinations" in client-facing communications
- Lack of clear policy on what was/wasn't allowed

**The result:** Paralysis. No official AI usage, but uncontrolled experimentation happening in the shadows.

## The Risk They Faced

Their head of compliance discovered:

- 23 employees had used ChatGPT for work tasks
- 8 had uploaded client information to AI tools
- No tracking of what data had been shared
- No policies governing AI usage
- No incident response plan if something went wrong

**They needed governance fast—but didn't know where to start.**

## The Approach

We designed a 6-week sprint to establish "good enough" governance that would:

1. ✅ Enable AI adoption (not block it)
2. ✅ Protect client data and meet compliance requirements
3. ✅ Give leadership confidence to move forward

### Week 1: Risk Assessment & Requirements

**Activities:**

- Interviewed compliance team, advisors, and technology leaders
- Reviewed existing data security and compliance policies
- Identified regulated use cases vs. safe use cases
- Documented FINRA/SEC requirements

**Deliverable:** AI Risk Assessment Report

### Week 2: Framework Design

**Activities:**

- Designed three-tier use case categorization (Low/Medium/High risk)
- Created data classification rules specific to financial services
- Defined approval workflows for each risk tier
- Established human review checkpoints

**Deliverable:** AI Governance Framework (draft)

### Week 3: Policy Development

**Activities:**

- Drafted AI Usage Policy (2 pages, not 50)
- Created "Can I Use AI for This?" decision tree
- Developed use case approval form
- Wrote incident response playbook

**Deliverable:** Complete Policy Package

### Week 4: Tool Evaluation & Approval

**Activities:**

- Evaluated 5 AI tools requested by teams
- Assessed data privacy, security, and compliance features
- Approved 2 tools for immediate use, 1 for pilot, 2 rejected
- Documented approved use cases for each tool

**Deliverable:** Approved AI Tools List + Usage Guidelines

### Week 5: Training & Rollout

**Activities:**

- Conducted AI governance training for all employees (1-hour session)
- Created quick reference guides
- Set up approval workflow in existing ticketing system
- Appointed AI governance lead

**Deliverable:** Trained team + operational governance process

### Week 6: Monitoring & Iteration

**Activities:**

- Launched monitoring for approved tools
- Processed first use case approval requests
- Gathered feedback on policy usability
- Made quick adjustments based on real-world usage

**Deliverable:** Live governance program with continuous improvement

## The Solution

**A Practical Governance Framework for Financial Services:**

### 1. Three-Tier Risk Classification

**Low Risk (Pre-Approved):**

- Internal research and analysis (no client data)
- Drafting internal communications
- General market research
- Approved tools only

**Medium Risk (Manager Approval Required):**

- Client communication assistance (review before sending)
- Portfolio analysis and recommendations (advisor validates)
- Data analysis with anonymized client data

**High Risk (Compliance Review Required):**

- Automated client communications
- Algorithmic trading decisions
- Regulatory filings
- Anything involving direct client PII

### 2. Data Protection Rules

**Strict boundaries established:**

- ❌ Never upload raw client PII to external AI tools
- ❌ Never use AI for final compliance decisions without review
- ✅ Anonymize data before using AI analysis
- ✅ Use approved enterprise AI tools with data protection agreements
- ✅ All AI outputs must be reviewed by qualified professional

### 3. Monitoring & Accountability

**Implemented tracking:**

- Monthly review of AI tool usage
- Quarterly policy effectiveness assessment
- Incident reporting mechanism
- Regular spot-checks of AI-assisted work products

## Key Takeaways

- **Governance doesn't have to take 6 months** — 6 weeks is enough to start safely
- **Perfect is the enemy of good** — Better to have "good enough" governance today than perfect governance never
- **Financial services compliance is manageable** — With the right framework, you can adopt AI safely in regulated industries
- **Start with use case tiers** — Not all AI usage carries the same risk
- **Enable, don't just restrict** — Good governance says "yes" safely, not just "no"
