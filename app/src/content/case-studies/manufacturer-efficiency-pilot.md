# How a 250-Person Manufacturer Increased Production Efficiency 23% in 90 Days

## The Challenge

A precision parts manufacturer with 250 employees was struggling with production scheduling inefficiencies. Their production planners were manually juggling:

- 12 production lines
- 150+ active orders at any given time
- Complex dependencies between jobs
- Frequent rush orders disrupting the schedule
- Machine maintenance windows
- Labor availability constraints

**The result:**

- 18% average machine downtime
- Frequent schedule conflicts and delays
- Production planners spending 15+ hours/week on schedule adjustments
- Customer complaints about delivery delays

**They knew they had an AI opportunity, but weren't sure where to start.**

## The Approach

We began with a 2-week assessment to understand their current state:

### Week 1-2: Discovery

- Shadowed production planners to understand workflow
- Analyzed 6 months of production data
- Identified scheduling patterns and pain points
- Calculated baseline metrics (downtime, throughput, labor hours)

**Key Finding:** 60% of schedule conflicts were predictable based on historical patterns.

### Week 3-4: Pilot Design

- Selected one production line (12 machines) for pilot
- Defined success metrics and baselines
- Chose commercial scheduling AI tool (no custom development)
- Set up data integration with existing ERP system

### Week 5-8: Implementation

- Deployed AI scheduling assistant
- Trained 3 production planners on new workflow
- AI suggests optimal schedules, planners review and approve
- Daily refinement based on feedback

### Week 9-12: Measurement & Optimization

- Collected performance data
- Fine-tuned AI parameters
- Expanded to second production line
- Documented ROI for leadership presentation

## The Solution

**AI-Assisted Production Scheduling:**

Instead of replacing human planners, we augmented them with AI:

1. **AI analyzes historical data** to predict optimal schedules
2. **AI flags potential conflicts** before they happen
3. **AI suggests schedule adjustments** when rush orders arrive
4. **Human planners review and approve** all AI recommendations

**Key principle:** Keep humans in control, let AI handle the complexity.

## The Results

### After 90 Days:

**Efficiency Gains:**

- ✅ **23% reduction in machine downtime** (18% → 13.9%)
- ✅ **15% increase in throughput** (same labor, more output)
- ✅ **85% reduction in scheduling labor** (15 hours → 2.25 hours/week)

**Financial Impact:**

- **Increased production value:** ~$280K/year (15% throughput increase)
- **Labor savings:** $31K/year (12.75 hours/week × $48/hour fully loaded)
- **Reduced downtime value:** $29K/year (fewer delays and rush charges)
- **Total annual value:** ~$340K/year

**Investment:**

- **Pilot cost:** $35K (assessment + implementation + training)
- **Annual software cost:** $12K
- **First-year ROI:** 871%
- **Payback period:** 6 weeks

### Qualitative Benefits:

- **Reduced stress** for production planners ("I actually go home on time now")
- **Improved customer satisfaction** (fewer delivery delays)
- **Better machine utilization** (more predictable maintenance windows)
- **Foundation for expansion** (ready to scale to all production lines)

## What Made This Work

### ✅ Started with Clear Business Problem

Not "let's use AI in manufacturing" but "we need to reduce scheduling conflicts and downtime"

### ✅ Chose Contained Pilot

One production line, not entire facility. Proved value before scaling.

### ✅ Measured from Day 1

Established baseline metrics before starting. Tracked weekly progress.

### ✅ Kept Humans in Control

AI assists, humans decide. This built trust and adoption.

### ✅ Used Commercial Tools

No need for custom AI development. Integrated with existing systems.
