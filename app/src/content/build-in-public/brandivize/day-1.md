## Okay, Before I Get Too Deep, Please Let Me Confess Something

Seriously, I have to confess something.

I've spent the last 4+ years studying copywriting and the great direct sales masters of last century.

So why is this a confession?

Because, that has completely changed how I view building businesses.

One of my favorite copywriters is Eugene Schwartz.

In my opinion he is one of the greatest marketers who has ever lived. But not because he had clever tactics, which he did, but rather he had this deep understanding of how people actually move toward making a buying decision (my favorite kind).

Fortunately for us, he documented what he learned by publishing 10 books.

One of his most important contributions was identifying how awareness works. He showed that buyers do not all hear the same message the same way. Where someone is mentally matters more than what you want to say.

One of his greatest contributions are his five stages of awareness. These perfectly describe a customer's journey.

Here they are..., and remember everyone goes through these stages:

**Unaware**  
We do not realize we have a problem, so nothing being offered feels relevant.

**Problem Aware**  
We feel something is wrong, but we do not know what would actually fix it.

**Solution Aware**  
We know there are ways to solve it, just not which approach or product makes sense.

**Product Aware**  
We know your product exists, but we still need a reason to choose it.

**Most Aware**  
We already want what you offer. We just need an easy way to buy it.

This framework is less about persuasion and more about timing. The strategy is to say the right thing, but only when the listener is ready to hear it.

Here’s a cleaner, simpler version in plain language, still human, still direct, less “marketing”:

## Discovering a Universal Problem

Alright, confession out of the way. Here’s the real issue a lot of businesses run into.

They are talking to the wrong people with the wrong message.

That sounds small, but it is not.

This one mistake causes companies to waste money, stall out, or slowly bleed until they shut down.

The frustrating part is this. Nothing is actually wrong with their product.

With a small change in how they talk about it, they suddenly start reaching the right person at the right time.

Let’s call this **our problem**.

> _Incorrect messaging._

And here are a few issues messaging misalignment creates.

- Sales stay low
- Finding customers is hard
- Growth in many cases becomes impossible
- Everything just becomes harder than it would be if sales were rocking

And that's just the surface.

When companies get the messaging aligned, money flows... and in business there are few problems that money can't solve.

## My Solution

So here's the high-level product idea.

**Core engine:**

- Stage of awareness detector (unaware to most aware)
- Market sophistication analyzer
- Desire/frustration mapping
- Messaging hierarchy builder (brand promise → product positioning → feature messaging → benefit articulation)
- Voice/tone consistency validator

**Key observation:** Most companies have fragmented messaging because they lack a systematic framework. They know their product but can't articulate the complete message architecture. This solves that by embedding the Breakthrough Advertising methodology into an executable system.

**Output:** Complete messaging framework document that teams can actually use - not generic AI content, but a structured message architecture based on proven direct response principles.

Point it at your website or paste in your marketing message → get back the same core message optimized for all 5 stages of awareness.

**The insight:** Most companies write for ONE awareness stage (usually "Product Aware" or "Solution Aware") and wonder why they're leaving money on the table. They're invisible to 80% of their potential market.

**How it works:**

1. **Input:** URL or paste current marketing copy
2. **Analysis:** System detects which awareness stage you're currently writing for
3. **Transformation:** Extracts your core value prop, benefits, mechanism
4. **Output:** Side-by-side comparison showing your message rewritten for all 5 stages:
   - Unaware: Problem identification, no mention of solutions yet
   - Problem Aware: Pain amplification, desire building
   - Solution Aware: Category education, your approach vs alternatives
   - Product Aware: Direct feature/benefit comparison
   - Most Aware: Urgency, specificity, offer optimization

**The "aha" moment:** They instantly see which audience segments they've been completely ignoring. And they get ready-to-deploy copy variations.
